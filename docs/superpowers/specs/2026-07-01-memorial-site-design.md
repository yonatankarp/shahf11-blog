# חיה בסרט(ן) — memorial site charter

## Why this exists

חיה wrote the blog *חיה בסרט(ן)* on Tapuz forums during her breast-cancer journey. She died on
19 May 2010. When Tapuz shut its forums down, her writing was lost with them. This project is an
attempt to bring it back and keep it, so the family and the people who loved her can still read
her in her own words.

## Who it is for

Close family first, and future family members who never met her, with friends as a possible wider
circle. It is not built for search engines, wide reach, or growth. It is built to be warm, legible,
and permanent. Because the audience is intimate, whether it stays on the open public web is a
deliberate open question, not a default.

## What it holds

Her 173 recovered posts as a readable chronological journal, a gallery of photos of her, and the
graduation film uploaded to her own YouTube account (*חיה בסרט(ן) - סרט גמר יא'*, 2009). Because
this project exists precisely because a platform disappeared, anything that lives only on someone
else's servers is treated as at-risk: the video is downloaded and preserved inside the repository,
not merely embedded.

## How the work is split

The visual design is owned by the author's sister, who will provide a Figma design for us to
implement. Nothing visual is built ahead of that Figma, because it would only be thrown away.

Phase 1, now, is the foundation that does not depend on the design: preserve the at-risk media
first (the video, then the photos), then finish the OCR cleanup so her words are readable and
faithful, and keep post metadata consistent so any layout can render it.

Phase 2, later, is implementing the sister's Figma: the landing page, the reading view for the
journal, the gallery, and the video section, built so content and styling stay separate.

## Sequencing decision

Her words are the heart of the project but they are not the part at risk right now, because the
scans and PDFs are already held safely. The video on YouTube is the fragile one. So Phase 1 starts
with preserving the video and photos, and the OCR cleanup follows as its own focused pass.
